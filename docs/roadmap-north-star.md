# BoundLore North Star Roadmap

Purpose: This is a long-term orientation map, not an immediate implementation mandate.

## Vision
BoundLore becomes the most useful Light No Fire knowledge platform by combining:
- high-volume, high-quality structured content,
- excellent usability and discoverability,
- active community moderation and contribution workflows,
- strong multimedia support,
- fast and legally robust technical operations,
- standout utility tools that go beyond a classic wiki.

## Priorities (Now / Next / Later)

### Now (Foundation)
- Content model readiness:
  - Keep article and post structures extensible for future metadata (version, source, patch tags, language).
- UX consistency:
  - Maintain clear navigation, mobile-first readability, and predictable page patterns.
- Contribution quality controls:
  - Strengthen moderation queue quality, reviewer workflows, and transparent publish criteria.
- Technical hygiene:
  - Preserve fast loading, avoid regressions, keep SEO and legal/privacy pages current.

### Next (Scale)
- Structured knowledge depth:
  - Expand category coverage with deeper cross-linking ("See also", related entities, prerequisites).
- Patch cadence workflow:
  - Introduce clear pre-update vs post-update labeling and update status indicators.
- Discovery pipeline maturity:
  - Improve submission templates and conversion of discoveries into polished category content.
- Community operations:
  - Add editor/community programs, recurring contribution events, and clearer role onboarding.

### Later (Differentiators)
- Advanced tools:
  - Build planners, trackers, and interactive map/locator-style experiences.
- Rich media expansion:
  - Add galleries, video embeds, and advanced interactive components.
- Multilingual strategy:
  - Prepare localized content architecture and translation workflows.
- Platform evolution:
  - Reassess hosting/platform path (current stack vs migration options) based on growth needs.

## Decision Filter (use for all feature requests)
Before implementing, ask:
1. Does this improve long-term content depth, quality, or discoverability?
2. Does this strengthen mobile UX and contribution workflows?
3. Does this avoid one-off complexity and stay modular?
4. Does this keep us fast, SEO-safe, and legally/privacy aligned?
5. Does this move us toward scalable tooling or multilingual readiness?

If at least 3 answers are "yes", prioritize. If fewer than 3, defer or redesign.

## Guardrails
- Do not overbuild early: ship foundation before heavy custom tooling.
- Prefer reusable modules over page-specific hacks.
- Keep moderation and community trust mechanisms transparent.
- Optimize for maintainability under frequent game updates.
